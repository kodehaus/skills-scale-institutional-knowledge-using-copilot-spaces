# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads plan and oversee testing activities to ensure quality standards are met before releases. They maintain test plans, coordinate quality gates, and work closely with Developers, Project Managers, and Release Managers to track defects and sign off on releases.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and execute comprehensive testing strategies (unit, integration, E2E)
- Maintain test plans and quality gates for releases
- Coordinate defect tracking and resolution with Developers
- Provide release sign-off based on quality criteria
- Establish and monitor test coverage and automation metrics
- Collaborate with DevOps on automated testing in CI/CD

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal production defects
- Reduce time to detect and resolve issues
- Increase test automation coverage and efficiency
- Maintain clear quality standards and acceptance criteria

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to discuss blockers and testing progress
- Sprint planning to define testing approach and quality gates
- Release readiness meetings with Release Manager and PM
- Weekly QA metrics reviews and defect triage sessions
- Collaboration with Developers on PRs requiring quality validation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure product usability and accessibility by developing wireframes, prototypes, and design systems. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to align user experience with business goals and technical feasibility.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Develop and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature definitions
- Work with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase engagement
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with business and technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation
- User research findings presentations
- Sprint planning to scope design work
- Handoff sessions to explain design specifications and interactions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and execute release activities, ensuring smooth deployments to production. They work closely with Developers, QA Leads, DevOps Engineers, and Project Managers to plan releases, manage deployment schedules, and ensure rollback strategies are clear.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure release readiness with QA sign-off and checklist completion
- Manage release communication to stakeholders and support teams
- Coordinate with DevOps on deployment execution
- Document and execute rollback strategies when needed
- Track release metrics and identify process improvements

### Goals
- Achieve reliable, low-risk deployments to production
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Ensure clear communication and coordination across teams
- Continuously improve release processes and automation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM, QA, and DevOps
- Go/no-go meetings before production deployments
- Release announcements to stakeholders and support
- Post-release retrospectives to capture learnings
- Daily syncs during release windows for critical deployments

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code, and deployment automation. They enable fast, reliable releases by providing the infrastructure and tools needed for continuous integration, testing, and deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Design, build, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and configuration management
- Implement deployment automation and orchestration
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Collaborate with Developers on build and deployment issues
- Work with QA Leads to integrate automated testing into pipelines

### Goals
- Reduce deployment friction and manual effort
- Improve build and deployment speed and reliability
- Enhance system observability and incident response
- Maintain secure, scalable infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning for infrastructure and tooling work
- Incident response and on-call escalations
- Architecture discussions with Developers
- Release coordination with Release Manager
- Weekly syncs on pipeline health and technical debt
